How to use the log

  1. After every sail, write the conditions first. Wind, current, visibility, and boat type explain why a skill felt easy or hard.
  2. Log the skill, not just the outing. "Tacked ten times and got stuck in irons twice" is more useful than "went sailing."
  3. Pick one next drill. A short, specific next rep turns each sail into a learning loop.
  4. Use the learning plan, practice tests, and simulator between sails to target the weak spots.
