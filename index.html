About the ASA 101 written test

ASA 101 (Basic Keelboat Sailing) combines a written exam with on-water skill signoff. The public ASA standard covers boat parts, sail controls, points of sail, helm commands, navigation rules, aids to navigation, required safety gear, weather judgment, crew overboard recovery, docking/mooring, and six knots.

This trainer drills the whole beginner map: identify points of sail, read sail trim and telltales, steer and trim, judge right of way, click expanded boat parts, recognize navigation aids, practice sound signals, practice safety and knots, drill docking and anchoring decisions, sail mission-based simulator scenarios, and take randomized practice tests scored against the 80% pass mark. Start with the ASA 101 checklist if you want the full curriculum in order.

When you are ready to move beyond exam prep, use the beginner gear guide for gloves, foulies, PFDs, and class kit, then explore podcasts, forums, Reddit communities, and boat marketplaces for the next stage.

The official textbook: Sailing Made Easy is the ASA's own book — the written test comes directly from it. Find it on Amazon.
